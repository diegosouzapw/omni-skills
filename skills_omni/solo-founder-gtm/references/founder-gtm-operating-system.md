# Founder GTM Operating System

Use this reference to turn broad GTM advice into a weekly operating loop for a solo founder.

## 1. Stage Diagnosis

Classify the founder into the stage that best matches current reality.

| Stage | Main question | Typical failure mode | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Validation | Will anyone pay for this painful problem? | confusing interest for demand | interviews, offers, first paid users |
| Traction | Is there one repeatable acquisition motion? | channel sprawl | one motion, basic funnel, qualification |
| Systematization | Can what works be measured and repeated? | founder bottleneck | documentation, automation, metrics |
| Leverage | Should the founder simplify, automate, or hire? | premature team complexity | transfer readiness, margin, process quality |

## 2. Weekly Founder Cadence

### Daily

- protect one deep-work block for product or messaging work
- respond to high-signal leads and active customers
- capture objections, churn reasons, and confusing onboarding moments

### Weekly

- review one dashboard
- review one experiment
- review one pipeline summary
- review one churn or support summary
- decide one thing to stop, one thing to improve, and one thing to automate

### Monthly

- review pricing reactions
- review channel effectiveness
- review founder time allocation
- review whether the current motion still fits the buyer and product complexity

## 3. Constraint-First Decision Rule

Pick one current bottleneck only:

- not enough qualified conversations
- weak activation
- weak conversion
- poor retention
- support overload
- founder capacity overload

Do not recommend a broad GTM overhaul if one bottleneck explains most of the current pain.

## 4. Simplify vs Automate vs Hire

### Simplify when

- the process changes every week
- metrics are unclear or contradictory
- the founder cannot describe the process consistently

### Automate when

- the process is repetitive
- the desired output is already known
- errors are low-risk or easy to catch
- there is an approval or rollback path

### Hire when

- demand is sustained
- the process is documented
- the founder can define success metrics for the role
- handoff quality can be measured

## 5. Heuristic Thresholds

These are practitioner heuristics, not universal rules.

- solo founders often struggle once calendar load is dominated by calls and follow-up
- founder-led sales usually breaks before product quality does if qualification is weak
- support load is often a product/onboarding issue before it is a staffing issue
- adding channels too early usually hides the real bottleneck instead of solving it

## 6. Expected Outputs

A good GTM recommendation for a solo founder should produce:

- current stage
- dominant GTM motion
- largest current bottleneck
- minimum weekly metrics set
- one 14-day experiment
- automation candidates with approval boundaries
- clear hire / do-not-hire recommendation
