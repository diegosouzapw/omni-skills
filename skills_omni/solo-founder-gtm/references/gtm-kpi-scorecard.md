# GTM KPI Scorecard

Use this scorecard for a weekly solo-founder GTM review.

## Minimum KPI Set

| KPI | What it answers | Simple formula / definition | Weekly question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Visitors or leads | Is top-of-funnel moving? | Count by channel | Which channels produced qualified attention? |
| Qualified leads | Are we attracting the right people? | Leads that match ICP and buying criteria | Did lead quality improve or worsen? |
| Activation rate | Do new users reach first value? | activated users / new signups | Is onboarding working? |
| Time to first value | How fast does value show up? | median time from signup to activation | Where is friction? |
| Demo booked rate | Does interest convert to conversation? | demos booked / qualified leads | Is the CTA working? |
| Demo-to-close rate | Are calls converting? | closed won / demos attended | Is qualification and messaging strong enough? |
| Sales cycle | How long does revenue take? | median days from qualified opp to close | Are deals stalling? |
| Churn rate | Are customers staying? | lost customers or lost MRR over period | Is retention becoming the bottleneck? |
| Revenue per founder-hour | Is founder time compounding? | revenue / founder hours spent on GTM | Should this task be automated or removed? |

## Recommended Weekly Cadence

1. Review one dashboard.
2. Compare this week against trailing 4-week average.
3. Identify one constraint only:
   - traffic
   - lead quality
   - activation
   - close rate
   - retention
   - founder bandwidth
4. Choose one experiment and one cleanup task.
5. Stop doing one low-signal activity.

## Stage-Based Red Flags

| Stage | Red flag |
| --- | --- |
| Validation | many conversations but no one pays |
| Early traction | traffic grows but activation is flat |
| Repeatability | more deals enter pipeline but follow-up slips |
| Scale without hiring | automations exist but founder still approves everything manually |

## Notes

- Keep formulas simple and consistent.
- Avoid adding metrics that do not change decisions.
- If definitions change, document the change before comparing trends.
