# Privacy and Outbound Compliance Notes

These notes are operational cautions, not legal advice.

## Core Principles

- collect the minimum personal data needed for the GTM motion
- be clear about sender identity and purpose
- honor opt-outs and suppression requests reliably
- avoid sensitive personal data unless truly necessary and appropriately handled
- do not assume a tool vendor makes a workflow compliant

## For Analytics

- avoid collecting unnecessary personal data
- define events around product behavior, not excessive identity detail
- review whether tracking choices match user expectations and local requirements

## For Outbound

- review the target geography before launching campaigns
- ensure unsubscribe handling is real, not cosmetic
- keep records of suppression choices
- avoid misleading subject lines or sender identities

## For AI Usage

- do not paste sensitive customer records into prompts unless strictly necessary
- redact names and details where possible
- review customer-facing outputs before allowing autonomous sending

## Route to Counsel When

- the founder asks whether a workflow is legally compliant
- the request depends on jurisdiction-specific interpretation
- the workflow involves sensitive categories of personal data
- the founder wants certainty rather than operational caution
