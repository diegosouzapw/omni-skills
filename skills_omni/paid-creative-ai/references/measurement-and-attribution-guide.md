# Measurement and Attribution Guide

Use this file before declaring a creative winner or loser.

## Questions to Confirm First

1. What conversion event is the platform optimizing toward?
2. What attribution window is being used?
3. Are view-through and click-through conversions both included?
4. Is the team comparing platform numbers with blended analytics, CRM, or finance numbers?
5. Are brand and non-brand effects separated where relevant?
6. Is reporting delayed because of sales lag or offline processing?

## Guardrails

- Platform reporting is useful for directional optimization, but it may not equal incremental business impact.
- Do not compare ROAS snapshots across platforms unless attribution settings and date windows are understood.
- When PMax or branded search is involved, check whether brand demand is inflating apparent creative success.
- When Meta windows differ from internal reporting windows, explain the mismatch before recommending budget shifts.

## Safe Interpretation Pattern

Use wording like:

- "Platform-reported performance suggests..."
- "This appears directionally stronger under the current attribution settings..."
- "Before reallocating budget, verify event definitions and reporting windows..."

Avoid wording like:

- "This creative definitively caused the revenue increase"
- "The platform winner is the true business winner"

## Escalation Cases

Escalate or route elsewhere when:

- tracking setup may be broken
- event duplication is suspected
- attribution settings are unknown
- CRM or finance disagreement is material and unresolved
