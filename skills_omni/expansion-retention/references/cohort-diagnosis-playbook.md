# Cohort Diagnosis Playbook

Use this playbook before recommending churn, expansion, or health-score changes.

## 1. Confirm data sources

Check what is available from:

- billing or subscription system
- CRM
- product analytics
- support platform
- customer-success platform
- survey or feedback systems

If data quality is weak, use manual account review and simple leading indicators instead of a complex score.

## 2. Segment the problem

Review retention by:

- signup or contract cohort
- plan or package
- ACV band
- pricing model
- acquisition motion
- customer segment
- geography if relevant

## 3. Locate the lifecycle break

Classify where the loss happens:

- onboarding failure
- weak adoption after first value
- stakeholder loss or poor account coverage
- renewal process failure
- billing or payment failure
- product-fit failure

## 4. Check activation and adoption

Ask:

- What is the first meaningful value event?
- What percentage of new customers reach it?
- Which milestones most strongly separate retained vs churned cohorts?
- Are support-heavy accounts also low-retention accounts?

## 5. Separate product churn from operational churn

Do not mix together:

- product dissatisfaction
- pricing mismatch
- procurement friction
- failed payment recovery issues
- renewal timing failure

## 6. Output shape

A useful diagnosis should include:

- affected cohorts and segments
- likely root causes
- confidence level
- missing data
- immediate interventions
- metrics to monitor over the next 30/60/90 days
