# Dunning and Renewal Escalation Template

Use this template when healthy or active customers are at risk due to payment or contract operations.

## Scenario type

- Failed payment
- Expired card
- PO / procurement delay
- Vendor onboarding delay
- Signature / contract admin issue
- Other:

## Ownership

- CS owner:
- Finance / billing owner:
- Sales / AE owner:
- Legal / procurement owner if needed:

## Dunning / escalation sequence

| Day | Trigger | Owner | Action | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Payment failure or admin issue detected | Billing / system | Immediate notice with clear next step | Make issue visible quickly |
| 3 | No resolution | Finance / CS | Reminder with payment or procurement instructions | Remove friction |
| 5 | Still unresolved | CS | Human outreach to confirm business impact and contacts | Prevent silent churn |
| 7 | Strategic account or high usage remains blocked | CS + Finance | Joint review and escalation | Protect revenue and relationship |
| 14 | Renewal or access risk remains | Leadership | Executive or commercial escalation | Prevent avoidable loss |

## Notes

- Keep product-risk workflow separate from billing-risk workflow.
- Use clear, non-threatening language.
- For strategic accounts, involve a human before access loss becomes the first serious signal.
- If procurement is the blocker, document exact requirements and dates rather than treating it as disengagement.
