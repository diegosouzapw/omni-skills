# Format Selection Guide

Choose the format based on audience, decision urgency, and evidence depth.

## 1. Executive brief

Use when:

- the audience is leadership or a decision-maker
- the reader needs a fast summary
- the recommendation matters more than raw detail

Required sections:

- purpose
- executive summary
- key findings
- recommendation
- risks or open questions
- references

## 2. Research summary

Use when:

- the task focuses on one question or topic
- the audience needs understanding more than immediate decision
- the evidence set is moderate in size

Required sections:

- question or scope
- summary of findings
- supporting evidence
- gaps or contradictions
- references

## 3. Decision comparison

Use when:

- multiple options must be compared
- tradeoffs need to be visible
- a recommendation should be tied to criteria

Required sections:

- decision context
- options compared
- comparison criteria
- evidence by option
- tradeoffs and risks
- recommendation
- references

## 4. Comprehensive report

Use when:

- the audience needs deep context
- the source set is broad or fragmented
- the report may be updated over time

Required sections:

- purpose and audience
- executive summary
- background/context
- detailed findings
- contradictions and uncertainty
- recommendations or next steps
- open questions
- references

## Quick selection rule

If the reader must decide quickly, start with an executive brief.
If the reader must compare paths, use a decision comparison.
If the reader must understand a topic deeply, use a research summary or comprehensive report.
