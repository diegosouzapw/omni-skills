# Quality Review Checklist

Use this before handoff or publication.

## Scope and audience

- Is the purpose of the document clear?
- Is the audience clear?
- Does the format match the user’s need?

## Evidence quality

- Does every important factual claim have a source?
- Are critical facts anchored to direct quotes or tight paraphrases?
- Are stale or conflicting sources flagged?
- Are inaccessible sources called out rather than ignored?

## Provenance

- Are inline citations present where needed?
- Does the references section include title, URL, and retrieved date?
- Is evidence clearly separated from inference and recommendation?

## Writing quality

- Does the summary appear before detail?
- Are headings meaningful?
- Are recommendations actionable?
- Are open questions explicit?

## Publication safety

- If the report is long, was it published incrementally?
- If the page was updated, was the update intent or changelog captured?
- Are links working and readable?
