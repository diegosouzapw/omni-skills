# Executive Brief Example

## Title

Onboarding Documentation Fragmentation in Notion

## Purpose

Provide a decision-ready summary of why onboarding is slow and inconsistent.

## Executive Summary

Onboarding guidance is split across HR, regional, and engineering pages, which creates duplicated instructions and unclear ownership. The strongest evidence points to process fragmentation rather than a single tooling failure.

## Key Findings

1. Regional teams maintain local variants of the onboarding checklist, leading to inconsistent steps and timing. [People Ops onboarding guide, retrieved 2026-03-27]
2. Engineering access requests occur after account creation rather than as part of a unified workflow. [Engineering new hire setup, retrieved 2026-03-27]
3. The central overview does not clearly define a cross-functional owner for end-to-end onboarding. [HR process overview, retrieved 2026-03-27]

## Recommendation

Assign one owner for end-to-end onboarding and consolidate the canonical checklist into one maintained page. This recommendation is a judgment based on the source pattern, not a direct quote from a source.

## Risks and Open Questions

- Some regional pages may be stale.
- Ownership might be documented outside Notion.
- Additional evidence from operations pages may still be needed.
