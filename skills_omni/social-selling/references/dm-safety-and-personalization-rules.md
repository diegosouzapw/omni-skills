# DM Safety and Personalization Rules

Use this guide for connection requests, first DMs, and follow-ups.

## Core rules

1. Personalize with real context.
2. Keep the first ask small.
3. Do not misrepresent familiarity.
4. Do not pressure for a meeting too early.
5. Stop after repeated non-response.
6. Move to nurture rather than forcing continuation.

## Personalization checklist

Reference one of the following:

- their recent post or comment
- a visible company event
- role change or hiring signal
- shared connection or alumni path
- a relevant problem they publicly discuss

Avoid fake personalization such as vague compliments or generic “love what you’re doing.”

## Safe progression

### Connection request

Purpose: open the relationship, not pitch.

Good shape:

- short
- specific
- no hard sell

### First DM

Purpose: start a relevant conversation.

Good shape:

- thanks or context
- one observation
- one useful insight or question
- no heavy CTA

### Follow-up

Purpose: add relevance, not repeat the same ask.

Good shape:

- new context
- useful resource or perspective if genuinely relevant
- low-pressure close

## Stop conditions

Stop and return to nurture when:

- there are multiple non-responses
- the reply is negative or uninterested
- the account shows warning signs or restrictions
- the operator cannot honestly personalize further

## Review questions

- Would this message still make sense if the recipient knew nothing about the sender?
- Is the message truthful and specific?
- Is there only one ask?
- Would a reasonable professional consider this relevant rather than spam?
