# Sales Navigator Workflow

Use Sales Navigator as a research, prioritization, and alerting tool.

## Step 1: Define the ICP

Capture:

- industry
- company size
- geography
- target titles and seniority
- buying trigger or pain
- disqualifiers

## Step 2: Build the account list

Start with accounts that fit the ICP. Favor clarity over list size.

Prioritize accounts with signs such as:

- recent hiring in the target function
- leadership changes
- expansion or funding events
- active discussion of the problem space
- existing relationship paths such as alumni or shared connections

## Step 3: Build the lead list

Use lead criteria tied to the buying motion:

- job title and seniority
- likely owner of the problem
- visible activity or relevance signals
- relationship proximity where available

## Step 4: Save searches and monitor alerts

Create saved searches for:

- high-fit accounts
- new leaders in relevant functions
- recently active prospects
- expansion or hiring signals
- relationship-based segments such as alumni or shared connections

Review alerts on a recurring cadence rather than rebuilding lists from scratch each day.

## Step 5: Prioritize outreach

Rank prospects using:

1. fit
2. timing or trigger event
3. relationship warmth
4. proof relevance

Use the highest-fit, highest-timing prospects for fully manual outreach first.

## Step 6: Log to CRM

At minimum, capture:

- account
- lead
- source = LinkedIn / Sales Navigator
- trigger event
- outreach date
- response outcome
- meeting booked
- opportunity status

## Example prospecting recipes

### New leader recipe

Use when a new VP or Head joins an ICP account.

- account fits ICP
- title indicates ownership of the problem
- trigger = role change
- outreach angle = first 90 days priorities

### Hiring spike recipe

Use when a company is growing the relevant team.

- account fits ICP
- trigger = hiring volume in target function
- outreach angle = scaling pain, process consistency, enablement needs

### Active poster recipe

Use when a prospect regularly posts about the category.

- visible activity suggests relevance and a higher chance of recognition
- engage first, then message with context

### Relationship path recipe

Use when there is alumni or shared-connection overlap.

- start with the authentic commonality
- avoid overstating familiarity

## Avoid

- treating Navigator as a scraping tool
- assuming exact product filters or features will stay constant
- pushing list volume before verifying fit and messaging quality
