# Profile Audit Checklist

Use this checklist before scaling outreach or content. A weak profile reduces acceptance, reply quality, and trust.

## 1. Positioning

- Is the profile clearly written for the target buyer, not just as a resume?
- Does the headline state audience, outcome, and angle clearly?
- Does the About section explain the problem, method, proof, and next step?
- Is the CTA visible and appropriate for the user’s sales motion?

## 2. Credibility

- Professional photo appropriate for the market
- Banner reinforces positioning or proof
- Experience section highlights outcomes, not only responsibilities
- Featured section includes proof assets such as case study, article, customer story, talk, framework, or booking page
- Skills and recommendations support the stated positioning

## 3. Buyer Relevance

- The first screen answers “who is this for?” and “why should I care?”
- Language matches buyer pain and category terms the ICP uses
- Claims are specific and supportable
- Tone sounds credible, not inflated or gimmicky

## 4. Profile Hygiene

- Public profile URL is customized
- Contact options are present and current
- Featured links work
- Recent activity does not contradict the target positioning
- Deprecated platform advice is removed

## 5. Rewrite Priorities

If the profile is underperforming, rewrite in this order:

1. Headline
2. About section opening lines
3. Featured proof
4. Experience outcomes
5. CTA and contact path

## Drafting pattern

### Headline formulas

- I help `[ICP]` achieve `[outcome]` through `[method]`
- `[Role]` for `[audience]` | `[proof or angle]`
- Helping `[audience]` solve `[problem]` without `[common pain]`

### About structure

1. Problem the ICP recognizes
2. How the user approaches it
3. Proof, examples, or outcomes
4. Low-friction next step

## Output expected from an audit

- top 3 profile weaknesses
- rewritten headline options
- revised About draft
- proof assets to add to Featured
- one recommended CTA
