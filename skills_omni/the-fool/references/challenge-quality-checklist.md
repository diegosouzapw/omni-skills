# Challenge Quality Checklist

Use this to improve signal before presenting critique.

## A strong challenge should be

- tied to the steelmanned thesis
- specific rather than generic
- consequential rather than trivial
- grounded in observation, reasoning, or evidence state
- clear about uncertainty
- tagged as assumption-risk, evidence-risk, execution-risk, or adversarial-risk

## Quick review questions

- Is this challenge materially different from the others?
- Would this objection still matter if the thesis were presented by a highly competent team?
- Did I identify what evidence would reduce uncertainty?
- Am I criticizing the thesis, not the user?
- Am I staying inside critique scope instead of solving the problem?
- Would a decision-maker learn something useful from this objection?

## Anti-patterns

- vague "what if" questions
- obvious edge cases with little impact
- repeating the same risk in different wording
- confidence language stronger than the evidence supports
- drifting into implementation planning
