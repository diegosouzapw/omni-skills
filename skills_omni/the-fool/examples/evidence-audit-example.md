# Evidence Audit Example

## User request

> Challenge our claim that users want an AI copilot because 14 interviewees said they would use it.

## Critique excerpt

**Steelmanned thesis:**  
You believe early customer interviews provide enough signal to justify prioritizing an AI copilot because repeated positive feedback indicates meaningful demand.

**Mode used:**  
Test the Evidence

**Challenge 1: Stated intent is being treated as adoption proof** `[evidence-risk]`  
- **Observation:** Support comes from 14 interviews describing future interest.
- **Inference:** Reported enthusiasm is being used as evidence of actual workflow adoption.
- **Why it matters:** Users often overstate willingness to use AI features before confronting trust, latency, and workflow friction.
- **What evidence would reduce uncertainty:** Prototype usage with representative tasks.

**Challenge 2: The sample may not represent the target segment** `[assumption-risk]`  
- **Observation:** No segment mix or recruitment method is provided.
- **Inference:** A skewed sample may be inflating demand.
- **Why it matters:** Building for vocal power users can mislead roadmap priority.
- **What evidence would reduce uncertainty:** Segment-balanced testing and usage behavior.

**Overall confidence:**  
LOW

**Next validation action:**  
Run a task-based prototype and compare completion, trust, and repeat usage before committing roadmap capacity.
