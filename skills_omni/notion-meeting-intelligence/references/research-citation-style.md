# Research Citation Style

Use this style whenever external research is added to a meeting packet.

## Sectioning

Separate content into:
- Internal context
- External research
- Assumptions or proposals
- Open questions

## Citation rules

- Every external factual claim should include a link.
- Keep citations close to the claim they support.
- If a statement is synthesis rather than a direct source fact, label it as analysis or recommendation.
- Do not present external benchmarks as internal policy or approved direction.

## Example

- Internal context: The support team reported a 14% rise in backlog after the workflow change. [Notion: Weekly support review]
- External research: Industry case studies often show adoption drag when automation launches without exception routing. [Source link]
- Assumption: If exception handling remains manual, rollout risk is likely to stay elevated.
- Open question: Which failure modes must be reviewed live in the meeting?
