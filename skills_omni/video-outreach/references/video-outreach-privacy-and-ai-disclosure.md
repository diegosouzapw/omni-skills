# Video Outreach Privacy and AI Disclosure

Use this checklist before launching a campaign that uses personalization, screenshots, or AI-generated media.

## Privacy checklist

- Collect only the prospect and company information needed to make the outreach relevant.
- Avoid sensitive personal data.
- Avoid personalization that feels invasive, such as referencing information that is technically public but contextually creepy.
- Limit who can access prospect screenshots, recordings, and generated assets.
- Remove campaign-specific screenshots and generated media when they are no longer needed.
- Keep retention periods short and operationally justified.

## AI likeness and synthetic media checklist

- Confirm you have authorization before cloning a real person's face or voice.
- Review whether the outreach could mislead the recipient about how the video was made.
- If the fact that the video is AI-generated would materially affect trust, disclose it clearly.
- Test pronunciation, lip sync, and dynamic fields before sending at scale.
- Have a fallback human-recorded option ready for trust-sensitive segments.

## Safe personalization guidance

Good personalization usually includes:

- company name
- role-relevant business context
- a visible website or product reference
- one recent trigger event if relevant
- a useful artifact tied to their likely priorities

Avoid using:

- sensitive categories of personal data
- deeply personal references unrelated to the business problem
- screenshots or data dumps that reveal more than necessary

## Retention prompts

Decide and document:

- where source screenshots are stored
- who can access uploaded voice, avatar, or replica assets
- when campaign assets are deleted or archived
- whether vendor-hosted generated media is retained by default

## Disclosure prompts

Use disclosure when it would improve honesty or reduce confusion, for example:

- when using AI avatars instead of a real recording
- when cloning a sender's voice or likeness
- when the recipient is likely to assume the sender recorded the clip manually

Keep disclosures simple and non-defensive.
