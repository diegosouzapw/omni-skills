# Video Outreach Decision Matrix

Use this matrix before recommending a tool or sequence.

## Quick routing

| If this is true | Prefer | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fewer than 20 videos per week and high-value accounts | Human-recorded video | Highest authenticity and easiest quality control |
| 50-500 videos per month and moderate to high ACV | Human-led video with AI assistance | Balances personalization and efficiency |
| 500+ videos per month and strong process maturity | AI-personalized video | Scale justifies automation if trust risks are managed |
| The buyer needs to see the product more than the sender | Interactive demo plus optional short intro video | Better for self-education and internal sharing |
| Post-demo recap, proposal walkthrough, or champion enablement | Human-recorded screen share | Clarity and trust matter more than scale |

## Decision factors

| Factor | Human-recorded | AI-personalized | Interactive demo |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Authenticity | Highest | Medium to low unless carefully disclosed and reviewed | Medium-high if framed as product education |
| Scale | Low | High | Medium-high |
| Effort per send | High | Low after setup | Medium |
| Best use case | High-value cold outreach, recap, proposal | Volume outbound, multilingual scale | Async selling, pre-demo education |
| Main risk | Time cost | Trust backlash, quality issues, disclosure risk | Too much friction or product complexity |
| Must-have ops | Thumbnail, hosting, CTA, CRM note | Approval, QA, tracking, disclosure policy | Demo analytics, CRM sync, clear path to CTA |

## Practical guidance

### Choose human-recorded video when

- the account is strategic
- the outreach relies on authenticity or executive trust
- the message references specific context that should not sound templated
- the sender is uncomfortable with synthetic media risk

### Choose AI-personalized video when

- the volume is too high for true 1:1 recording
- the team can quality-check names, pronunciation, and render outputs
- there is a clear approval path for cloned face, voice, or avatars
- the campaign can tolerate imperfect trust signals in exchange for scale

### Choose an interactive demo when

- the product itself is the value story
- a short guided experience is more persuasive than talking-head video
- internal forwarding is likely
- the buyer wants to explore before booking time

## Red flags

Do not recommend AI-personalized outreach if:

- the user wants to impersonate a real person without authorization
- the campaign depends on pretending each video was manually recorded when it was not
- there is no QA process for names, script fields, or rendering checks
- the segment is highly trust-sensitive and the team has not tested reactions carefully

## Minimum data to collect first

- volume per week or month
- average deal size or account importance
- target buyer role
- languages required
- CRM and sequencing stack
- whether analytics and hosting are available
- whether AI likeness use is approved internally
