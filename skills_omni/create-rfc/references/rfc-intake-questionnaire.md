# RFC Intake Questionnaire

Use these questions when the user has not provided enough context to draft a credible RFC.

Ask only what is missing.

## Core questions

1. What change are you proposing?
2. What problem does it solve?
3. Why is this decision needed now?
4. What happens if we do nothing?
5. Who is driving this RFC?
6. Who can approve or reject the decision?
7. Which teams, systems, customers, or processes are affected?
8. What is the impact level: low, medium, or high?
9. Is there a target decision date or deadline?
10. Are there known constraints such as budget, headcount, compliance, timing, or platform limitations?

## Option quality questions

1. What options are already under consideration?
2. Which option seems preferred today, if any?
3. What would a credible status quo or do-nothing option look like?
4. Are there any options that were considered and ruled out early? Why?

## Evidence questions

1. What data, incidents, costs, research, or customer feedback support the need for change?
2. Are there current pain points with numbers or examples?
3. Do we have prior experiments, postmortems, benchmarks, or vendor evaluations?

## Assumptions and risk questions

1. What assumptions must be true for the preferred option to succeed?
2. How confident are we in each major assumption?
3. What would invalidate those assumptions?
4. What are the major risks if we choose the wrong option?

## Decision quality questions

1. What criteria will determine the best option?
2. Which criteria are must-haves versus nice-to-haves?
3. Are some criteria more important than others?
4. How will we measure success if the RFC is approved?

## Follow-up questions

1. Will this need a later TDD or implementation plan?
2. Are there linked issues, milestones, tickets, or deadlines?
3. Does the RFC supersede an earlier proposal or decision?
