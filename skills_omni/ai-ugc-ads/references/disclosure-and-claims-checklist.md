# Disclosure and Claims Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing creator-led or AI-assisted UGC.

## 1. Sponsorship and material connection

Check whether the creator received:

- payment
- free product
- affiliate commission
- discount, trip, gift, or access
- any other incentive tied to coverage

If yes, the relationship likely needs clear disclosure.

## 2. Disclosure quality

Confirm that disclosure is:

- clear and hard to miss
- close to the endorsement, not buried
- understandable in the target market
- present in both the content and caption where appropriate
- not hidden behind vague tags or long hashtag blocks

Examples of clearer disclosure patterns include straightforward labels such as paid partnership or ad-style language, subject to platform and market rules.

## 3. Testimonial truthfulness

Do not present statements as genuine customer experience unless they are truthful and authorized.

Verify:

- the speaker actually used the product if that is implied
- quoted outcomes are real and attributable
- before/after stories are not fabricated
- edited clips do not distort the original meaning
- testimonials do not imply typical results unless support exists

## 4. Claims substantiation

For every explicit or implied claim, ask:

- can the brand support this claim with evidence?
- is the claim too absolute or guaranteed?
- does the creative imply medical, financial, or legal outcomes?
- does a visual imply a result stronger than the spoken words?

Escalate for human review when claims involve:

- health, supplements, skincare, weight loss, or medical outcomes
- finance, credit, investing, or earnings
- regulated services or age-restricted products

## 5. Synthetic media and AI risk

Do not use AI media deceptively.

Verify:

- the avatar, likeness, or voice is licensed or authorized
- synthetic content is not impersonating a real person without permission
- AI-generated testimonials are not presented as genuine customer stories
- the media does not materially mislead viewers about who is speaking or what was experienced

## 6. Rights and reuse

Confirm:

- usage rights duration
- channels covered: organic, paid social, website, email, marketplace, retail
- whether raw footage is included
- whether editing, subtitles, localization, and derivatives are allowed
- whether paid amplification rights are included

## 7. Platform-specific checks

Before launch, confirm:

- TikTok Spark Ads authorization requirements are met when needed
- Meta partnership ad or branded content approvals are complete when needed
- YouTube sponsorship disclosures are handled when applicable
- platform ad policies for the category have been reviewed

## 8. Stop conditions

Pause launch and escalate if:

- disclosure requirements are unclear
- a testimonial cannot be verified
- claims lack evidence
- an AI avatar or voice may be deceptive
- the content falls in a regulated category and no human reviewer has approved it
