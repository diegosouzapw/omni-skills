# UGC Metrics and Decision Framework

Use this worksheet to turn creative reporting into decisions without relying on stale universal benchmarks.

## Principle

Metrics are context-dependent. Interpret them against:

- account history
- platform and placement
- objective
- funnel stage
- offer strength
- landing-page quality
- audience saturation

## Recommended fields per experiment

Track:

- experiment ID
- date range
- platform and placement
- objective
- creator type: human, AI, hybrid, customer
- content format
- hook type
- body type
- CTA type
- spend
- impressions
- views or watch metrics available
- clicks
- conversions or downstream actions
- approval or rejection outcome
- next action

## Diagnostic interpretation

### Weak opening metrics, acceptable downstream economics unavailable

Likely issue:

- hook or first-scene weakness

Next move:

- keep the rest stable and test new openings

### Acceptable opening metrics, poor click or action rate

Likely issue:

- message-body mismatch
- weak product explanation
- weak CTA

Next move:

- test body structure or CTA before replacing creators

### Good click rate, poor conversion efficiency

Likely issue:

- landing-page mismatch
- offer weakness
- audience mismatch
- policy-limited wording

Next move:

- check post-click experience before discarding creative

### Results decline after initial success

Likely issue:

- fatigue or audience saturation

Next move:

- refresh hooks, intros, creators, or concept packaging in that order

## Sample decision rules

Use these as placeholders to calibrate, not universal truths:

- scale only after multiple days of stable efficiency relative to the account's target
- kill or rework only after enough spend or impression volume to reduce noise
- avoid major budget jumps that erase causal learning
- log why each creative was scaled, paused, refreshed, or retired

## Reporting cadence

### Daily

- identify delivery blockers, rejections, broken permissions, or obvious losers

### Weekly

- review winning hooks, creator patterns, fatigue signals, and trend opportunities

### Monthly

- compare creator cohorts, rights costs, AI vs human performance, and refresh velocity

## Decision owners

Assign an owner for each area:

- creative strategy
- creator operations
- compliance review
- paid media
- post-click conversion analysis

Without ownership clarity, teams often misdiagnose creative problems that are really permissions or funnel problems.
