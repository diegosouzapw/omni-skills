# Metric Selection by Motion

Use these as starter sets, not universal templates.

## 1. PLG AI product

### Good north-star candidates

- activated accounts
- weekly successful workflows
- retained usage by cohort

### Common driver metrics

- signup to activation rate
- TTFV
- feature adoption depth
- free-to-paid conversion
- PQL conversion rate
- gross margin after AI costs

### Guardrails

- support burden per account
- cost per successful action
- logo churn

## 2. Sales-led AI product

### Good north-star candidates

- net new ARR
- qualified pipeline created
- revenue from retained and expanding accounts

### Common driver metrics

- pipeline coverage
- win rate
- sales cycle length
- CAC payback
- slippage rate
- speed-to-lead for inbound demand

### Guardrails

- burn multiple
- GRR
- data health score

## 3. Hybrid PLG + sales-assisted motion

### Good north-star candidates

- activated and sales-qualified accounts
- expansion-ready product-qualified accounts

### Common driver metrics

- activation rate
- PQL volume
- PQL-to-opportunity conversion
- TTFV
- win rate on product-originated pipeline
- NRR by cohort

### Guardrails

- handoff delay from product to sales
- attribution disagreement between product and CRM systems

## 4. Agent-led or AI SDR motion

### Good north-star candidates

- qualified meetings created
- qualified pipeline influenced or created

### Common driver metrics

- send-to-reply ratio
- reply-to-meeting conversion
- cost per meeting booked
- cost per qualified lead
- human-to-AI leverage ratio
- win rate on AI-created pipeline

### Guardrails

- attribution tagging coverage
- meeting quality rate
- opt-out or complaint rate if tracked

## 5. Usage-based AI product

### Good north-star candidates

- retained consumed revenue
- successful usage volume

### Common driver metrics

- committed vs consumed revenue gap
- usage growth rate
- retained usage by cohort
- overage frequency
- revenue per consumption unit
- AI cost of revenue

### Guardrails

- cost per successful action
- gross margin after AI costs
- low-value usage inflation

## Selection rule

Choose the smallest metric set that can answer:

1. Are we creating value?
2. Are we converting value into revenue?
3. Are we doing so efficiently?
4. Is the growth durable?
5. Is the data trustworthy enough to act on?
