# Example: Attribution Dispute Resolution

## Scenario
A partner claims they sourced an opportunity after introducing the prospect in May. Direct sales says the account was already being pursued informally.

## Facts to inspect
- Was there a documented introduction?
- Was the opportunity already open in CRM before the introduction?
- Was there a valid registration under policy?
- Did the partner materially create or advance the buying process?
- Which timestamps and owner notes are authoritative?

## Example decision
- Credit as **partner-influenced**, not **partner-sourced**, because the account existed in active pursuit before the introduction.
- Preserve influenced reporting so the partner receives recognized contribution.
- Deny sourced-credit payout if the policy requires origination and registration that did not occur.

## Policy update
Add a rule clarifying that pre-existing active opportunities cannot become partner-sourced after the fact, but may become partner-influenced if the partner materially accelerates the deal.
