# Compliance Routing Matrix

This file is an operational routing aid, not legal advice.

Use it to identify when outreach planning can continue, when legal review is needed, and what records should be preserved.

## Core rule

If geography, recipient type, contact source, or message classification is unclear, pause legal conclusions and escalate.

## Intake questions

1. Which countries or regions are recipients in?
2. Is the outreach B2B, B2C, or mixed?
3. Is the message commercial or promotional?
4. How was recipient data obtained?
5. Is there an opt-out / unsubscribe process already in place?
6. Are suppression records preserved across tools?
7. Is profiling or sensitive inference being used for personalization?

## Routing table

| Situation | What this skill can do | Escalate when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| U.S.-focused commercial outreach | Summarize operational checks such as header accuracy, sender identity clarity, postal-address requirements, and opt-out processing expectations | the operator wants definitive legal interpretation or edge-case advice |
| UK outreach | Flag PECR and privacy review needs, ask whether consent or another lawful route has been assessed, and require opt-out handling clarity | the lawful basis or electronic-mail rule application is unclear |
| EU / EEA outreach | Flag GDPR/privacy implications and personalization/profiling sensitivity | the operator needs a legality decision, balancing test, or country-specific determination |
| Mixed-geo campaigns | Recommend segmentation by geography and separate review | one rule set is being applied blindly to all recipients |
| Third-party list purchase or unclear source | Treat as high risk and request provenance | provenance or permission basis cannot be explained |
| Suppression / unsubscribe uncertainty | Treat as a blocker to scale | removals are not processed reliably or cannot be evidenced |

## Minimum records to preserve

- contact source and acquisition notes
- segmentation by geography
- suppression and unsubscribe handling process
- date of last verification
- sender domain and mailbox ownership
- review notes when legal/compliance counsel is consulted

## What not to do

- do not state that a campaign is definitively lawful
- do not improvise country-by-country legal rules from memory
- do not equate “B2B” with “always allowed”
- do not ignore personalization/profiling concerns when signals are invasive or sensitive
