# Discovery Questionnaire

Use these prompts to gather the minimum facts needed for a motion recommendation.

## Product and user

1. What does the product do?
2. Who is the primary user?
3. Who is the economic buyer?
4. Can a new user reach value without human help?
5. What has to happen before a user experiences first value?

## Commercial context

6. What is the rough ACV or price band?
7. Is the company selling mostly to SMB, mid-market, enterprise, or mixed segments?
8. Is the current motion self-serve, sales-led, hybrid, or undefined?
9. What is the biggest current bottleneck: acquisition, activation, monetization, handoff, or expansion?

## Complexity and buying friction

10. Does the product require integrations, implementation, migration, or services before value is credible?
11. Are security, legal, or procurement reviews common?
12. How many stakeholders usually influence the deal?

## Evaluation path

13. Can buyers understand value through trial, freemium, demo, sandbox, audit, workshop, or ROI estimate?
14. What evaluation path exists today, and where does it break?
15. Can a user reach first value in minutes, hours, days, or weeks?

## Team and constraints

16. What GTM team exists today: founder-led, AE, SDR, CS, RevOps?
17. How much support can the team realistically provide?
18. Are there cost, abuse, or compliance constraints on free access?

## Assumptions template

If answers are missing, state assumptions like this:

- **Assumption:** buyers require light procurement review but users can start alone.
- **Impact on recommendation:** this pushes the motion toward hybrid rather than pure self-serve.
