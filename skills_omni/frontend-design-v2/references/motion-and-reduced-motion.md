# Motion and Reduced Motion

Motion should clarify, not dominate.

## Use Motion For

- enter and exit transitions
- state changes
- spatial orientation
- feedback after an action
- staged reveals when they support hierarchy

## Prefer

- transform and opacity
- short, intentional sequences
- easing that decelerates naturally
- one or two high-value motion moments per surface

## Avoid

- ambient decorative loops across the whole page
- bounce-heavy or novelty easing
- layout-triggering animation when a transform-based alternative exists
- motion that competes with reading or task completion

## Reduced Motion

Provide a reduced-motion path for non-essential effects.

Typical strategy:
- remove repeated decorative motion
- reduce distance traveled
- shorten duration
- replace elaborate choreography with simple fades or no motion

## Review Questions

- Does the motion communicate meaning?
- Can the same interface work with much less animation?
- Does reduced motion preserve usability and hierarchy?
- Are multiple moving elements firing at once without reason?
